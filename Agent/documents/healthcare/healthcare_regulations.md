# Healthcare Regulatory Framework — India


## CDSCO

Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation


Functions:

- Drug approval
- Manufacturing licence
- Quality control
- Medical device regulation
- Distribution control


## Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940

Regulates:

- Drugs
- Cosmetics
- Medical devices


## Medical Devices Rules, 2017

Risk-based classification system.

Requirements:

- Device labelling
- Manufacturer details
- Batch number
- Expiry date
- Storage conditions


## Schedule M GMP Requirements

Manufacturers must maintain:

- Quality management system
- Pharmacovigilance system
- Adverse drug reaction reporting


## Clinical Trials Framework

Governed by:

- Drugs and Cosmetics Act
- NDCT Rules 2019
- Medical Devices Rules 2017


### Ethics Committee

Requirements:

- CDSCO registration
- Human trial approval
- Five-year validity


### Informed Consent

Required:

- Written consent
- Patient information sheet
- Local language understanding


### Adverse Event Reporting

Serious adverse events:

24 hours reporting

Full analysis:

14 days


## Clinical Establishments Act 2010


Purpose:

Minimum standards for healthcare facilities.


Coverage:

Government and private healthcare facilities.


Implementation:

Health is State subject.


Adoption:

19 States/UTs


## Jan Vishwas Healthcare Reforms 2026


Changes:

- Criminal offences converted into penalties
- Graded penalties
- Hearing before penalty
- Appeal mechanism
