# Constitutional Framework — Property Law India

## Article 300A — Right to Property

Article 300A states:

"No person shall be deprived of his property except by authority of law."

Property right:

- Not a Fundamental Right.
- It is a Constitutional Right.
- Government acquisition must follow valid legal procedure.

Any arbitrary deprivation can be challenged before courts.

---

# Article 39(b) — Material Resources of Community

Directive Principle requiring the State to distribute material resources for common good.

Historically used to justify:

- Land acquisition
- Redistribution policies
- Welfare legislation

---

# Property Owners Association v. State of Maharashtra (2024)

Nine judge Constitution Bench judgment.

## Issue

Whether privately owned properties can be considered "material resources of community" under Article 39(b).

## Decision

Supreme Court held:

- Private property cannot automatically be treated as community resources.
- State cannot acquire private property without constitutional limitations.

Government action must satisfy:

- Article 14
- Article 19
- Article 21

## Importance

Strengthened protection of individual property rights.
