# New Criminal Laws India — BNS BNSS BSA 2023


## Criminal Law Reform Overview

India introduced major criminal law reforms effective from 1 July 2024.

Three laws replaced previous criminal statutes:


## Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023

Replaced:

Indian Penal Code, 1860


Purpose:

- Modern criminal offences
- Updated punishments
- Removal of colonial terminology


## Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023

Replaced:

Criminal Procedure Code, 1973


Purpose:

- Reform criminal investigation
- Introduce digital procedures
- Improve investigation timelines


## Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023

Replaced:

Indian Evidence Act, 1872


Purpose:

- Modern evidence framework
- Recognition of electronic evidence


# Police Powers Under BNSS


## Police Custody Period

Earlier CrPC:

Maximum police custody:

15 days


BNSS:

Police custody may extend:

- 60 days
- 90 days

Depending upon offence category.


## Multiple Custody Periods

BNSS allows police custody to be requested in multiple parts.

This means custody can be sought beyond the initial period within statutory limits.


## Handcuffing

BNSS provides statutory provisions for handcuffing in certain situations.

Applicable situations include:

- Serious offences
- Repeat offenders
- High-risk accused persons


## Section 172 BNSS — Preventive Action

Police can take action against persons who fail to follow lawful directions.

Possible actions:

- Detention
- Removal from place

Petty cases require release within 24 hours.
