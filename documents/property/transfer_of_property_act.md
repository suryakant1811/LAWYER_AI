# Transfer of Property Act, 1882

The Transfer of Property Act (ToPA) is the primary legislation governing transfer of immovable property.

Major transactions:

- Sale
- Mortgage
- Lease
- Exchange
- Gift
- Charges

---

# Sale — Section 54

## Definition

Sale means transfer of ownership in exchange for price.

Price may be:

- Paid
- Promised
- Part paid and part promised

---

## Important Rule

Contract of sale does not create ownership.

Ownership transfers only after:

- Execution of sale deed
- Registration

---

# Mortgage — Section 58

Mortgage means transfer of interest in property to secure repayment of debt.

Types:

1. Simple Mortgage
2. Conditional Sale Mortgage
3. Usufructuary Mortgage
4. English Mortgage
5. Equitable Mortgage

---

# Lease — Section 105

Lease transfers right to enjoy property for a fixed period.

Parties:

Lessor:
Owner giving property.

Lessee:
Person receiving right of enjoyment.

---

# Gift — Section 122

Gift means voluntary transfer of property without consideration.

Requirements:

- Existing property
- Voluntary transfer
- Acceptance by donee

---

# Exchange — Section 118

Exchange occurs when ownership of one property is transferred for another property.

Difference:

Sale = Money consideration

Exchange = Property consideration

---

# Charge — Section 100

Charge creates liability on property for repayment.

Difference:

Mortgage transfers interest.

Charge does not transfer ownership interest.
