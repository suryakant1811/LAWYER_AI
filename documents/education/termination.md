# TERMINATION & BUSINESS TRANSFERS

## Retrenchment of Workmen

Industrial Disputes Act requires:

- One month notice (or wages in lieu)
- Retrenchment compensation
- Last-in-first-out principle

Termination must be based on:

- Genuine business necessity

Victimisation amounts to:

- Unfair Labour Practice

---

## Misconduct Termination

Before dismissal:

- Domestic enquiry
- Opportunity of hearing
- Principles of natural justice

must be followed.

---

## Termination of Non-Workmen

Governed primarily by:

- Employment contract
- Company policy
- Shops & Establishments Act

Usually requires:

- One month notice
- Salary in lieu

---

## Business Transfer

Employees with continuous service generally retain benefits if:

- Employment continues uninterrupted
- Service conditions are not worsened

Otherwise retrenchment compensation may arise.

---

## Non-Compete Clauses

Indian Contract Act declares restraint of trade void.

Therefore:

- During employment → generally valid
- After employment → generally unenforceable
