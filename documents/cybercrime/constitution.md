# Constitutional Basis of Cyber Law

## Article 19(1)(a) — Freedom of Speech and Expression

Online speech, social media posts, blogs, emails and digital communication are protected under Article 19(1)(a).

Reasonable restrictions may be imposed under Article 19(2) in the interests of:

- Public Order
- Decency
- Morality
- Defamation
- Security of the State
- Sovereignty and Integrity of India

---

## Article 21 — Right to Privacy

Recognized as a Fundamental Right in:

K.S. Puttaswamy v Union of India (2017)

This forms the constitutional basis for:

- Data Privacy
- Cyber Stalking
- Revenge Porn
- Digital Arrest Scams
- Data Breaches

---

## Article 14

Protects against arbitrary blocking of websites, social media accounts and discriminatory application of cyber laws.

---

## Articles 32 & 226

Provide constitutional remedies through the Supreme Court and High Courts for challenging unconstitutional cyber laws or blocking orders.
