# Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023

## Criminal Law Reform

Effective:

1 July 2024

Three major laws replaced:

| Old Law | New Law |
|-|-|
| IPC 1860 | Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita |
| CrPC 1973 | Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita |
| Evidence Act 1872 | Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam |

---

# Police Custody Changes

## Earlier CrPC

Maximum police custody:

15 days

## BNSS

Police custody can extend:

- Up to 60 days
- Up to 90 days

depending on offence category.

---

# Multiple Police Custody

BNSS allows custody periods to be divided.

Police can seek custody during:

- First 40 days
- First 60 days

depending on offence.

---

# Section 172 BNSS

Allows police preventive action.

A person refusing lawful police directions may be:

- Detained
- Removed

---

# Handcuffing

BNSS provides statutory authority for handcuffing in specified situations.
