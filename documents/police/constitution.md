# Constitutional Framework — Police & Criminal Law India

## Article 21 — Right to Life and Personal Liberty

Article 21 of the Constitution of India protects individuals from arbitrary deprivation of life and personal liberty.

In police and criminal law context, Article 21 governs:

- Arrest procedures
- Detention powers
- Investigation methods
- Custodial treatment
- Protection against police abuse

The Supreme Court has repeatedly held that arbitrary arrest and illegal detention violate Article 21.

Police powers must follow:

- Fair procedure established by law
- Judicial oversight
- Protection of human dignity
- Constitutional safeguards


## Article 22 — Protection Against Arrest and Detention

Article 22 provides safeguards to arrested persons.

### Rights Under Article 22(1)

Every arrested person has:

- Right to know grounds of arrest
- Right to consult a lawyer
- Right to legal defence


### Article 22(2)

A person arrested must be produced before the nearest Magistrate within 24 hours.

Police cannot keep an arrested person beyond 24 hours without judicial authorization.


## Preventive Detention

Article 22(4)(b) recognizes preventive detention laws.

However, preventive detention must follow:

- Constitutional limitations
- Procedural safeguards
- Judicial review


## Article 311 — Protection of Police Personnel

Article 311 protects government servants including police officers.

It regulates:

- Dismissal
- Removal
- Reduction in rank
- Disciplinary proceedings

Police personnel cannot be removed without following prescribed procedure.


## Constitutional Principles of Police Powers

Police authority must balance:

1. Public safety
2. Crime prevention
3. Individual liberty
4. Fundamental rights
5. Accountability
